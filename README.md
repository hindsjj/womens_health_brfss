## Data Notes:
* Preconception Health: Population estimates were calculated for women ages 18-49.
* Unanimous Responses:  Unanimous responses (0.0%/100.0%) could not have confidence intervals (CI), estimated number of adults, nor RSE calculated.
* Low Sample Sizes:  Data with sample sizes under 50 are not reliable and were suppressed.
* Data Aggregation: Data collected for multiple years were aggregated and estimates calculated using appropriate weighting methods.

## Column Definitions:
* Years: The survey years for which aggregated population estimates were calculated.
* Status: The variable for which population estimates were calculated.
* Category: A general label for the category of grouping variables. 
* Group: The grouping variable for which population estimates were calculated.
* Region: State- or public health district-level data.
* Percent: The calculated prevalence of the Status variable within each Group.
* Lower/Upper_95CI: The calculated lower and upper 95% confidence intervals of the Percent value.
* Sample_Size: The total number of adults that responded to the survey for each combination of grouping and response variables. 
* Estimated_Adults: The estimated number of Idaho adults corresponding with the Percent value.
* Relative Standard Error (RSE): An index of variability defined as (Standard Error / Percent) x 100.  Estimates with RSE > 30 are considered unreliable by the CDC.  Estimates with RSE > 50 are considered very unreliable by the Idaho BRFSS.

## Additional Notes:
Data collected for a single year or had inconsistent definitions across years were excluded from this analysis.
